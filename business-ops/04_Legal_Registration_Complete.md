**4. File Incorporation Documents**

**For US C-Corp or LLC:**

**Documents Needed:**

**How to File:**

**Option 1: DIY**
- Download forms from state website

- **LegalZoom:** $149-$349 + state fees

- **Pros:** Faster, handles paperwork
- **Cons:** More expensive, some upselling

**Option 4: Lawyer**
- $1,500-$5,000
- **Pros:** Expert guidance, customized documents
- **Cons:** Expensive

**Recommended:** Stripe Atlas or Clerky for tech startups; LegalZoom/ZenBusiness for others

**Time:** 1-3 weeks

**5. Create Foundational Legal Documents**

#### A. Operating Agreement (LLC) or Bylaws (C-Corp)

**Operating Agreement (LLC):**
Defines internal operations, ownership, and decision-making.

**Key Sections:**
- Members and ownership percentages
- Voting rights
- Profit distributions
- Management structure (member-managed vs manager-managed)
- Transfer of ownership restrictions
- Dissolution terms

**Bylaws (C-Corp):**
Internal rules governing the corporation.

**Key Sections:**
- Shareholder meetings (annual meeting requirements)
- Board of directors (number, election, duties)
- Officers (CEO, CFO, Secretary roles)
- Stock issuance procedures
- Amendment procedures

**Where to Get:**
- Included with Stripe Atlas or Clerky
- Templates from SCORE or Rocket Lawyer
- Lawyer-drafted (best for complex situations)

---

#### B. Founder Agreement

**Purpose:** Prevent co-founder disputes - the #1 internal startup killer

**Critical Elements:**

**1. Equity Split**
```
Founder A: 50%
Founder B: 30%
Founder C: 20%
```

**Vesting Schedule:**
- 4-year vest with 1-year cliff (standard)
- Why: Protects company if someone leaves early

**2. Roles and Responsibilities**
- Who is CEO, CTO, COO, etc.?
- What are each person's primary responsibilities?
- Who has final say on what decisions?

**3. Decision-Making**
- What requires unanimous consent? (Raising money, selling company, bringing on co-founder)
- What requires majority? (Hiring, spending over $X)
- Who breaks ties?

**4. IP Assignment**
- All IP created by founders belongs to company
- Include "prior inventions" list (anything you created before company)

**5. Time Commitment**
- Full-time or part-time?
- What happens if someone wants to leave?
- Non-compete and non-solicit clauses (if enforceable in your state)

**6. Departure **
- Voluntary departure: unvested equity forfeited
- Termination for cause: all equity forfeited
- Company buyback of vested shares (at what price?)

**7. Dispute Resolution**
- Mediation first
- Arbitration if mediation fails
- Which state's laws govern

**Template Resources:**
- Founder Institute FAST Agreement
- YC founder agreement template
- Clerky
- Startup lawyer ($1,500-$3,000 for custom)

**‚ö†Ô∏è Critical:** Sign this BEFORE you start working together!

#### C. Stock Purchase Agreement / 83(b) Election

**For C-Corp Founders:**

**Stock Purchase Agreement:**
- Document showing founder purchased shares
- Usually at $0.0001/share (nominal price)
- Subject to vesting

**83(b) Election:**
- Tax form filed with IRS within 30 days of stock grant
- Elects to pay taxes NOW on current value (minimal) rather than later when company is worth more
- **Critical:** Missing 30-day deadline = potentially huge tax bill later

**Example:**
```
Without 83(b): If your unvested stock is worth $1M when it vests, you owe taxes on $1M income
With 83(b): You paid taxes on $100 value at grant, no tax when it vests, only pay capital gains on growth when you sell
```

**How to File:**
1. Fill out 83(b) election form
2. Mail to IRS (certified mail, keep receipt)
3. Include copy with your tax return
4. **Deadline:** 30 days from stock grant

**Resources:**
- Stripe Atlas includes 83(b) help
- Free templates online
- Tax advisor can help

**6. Obtain EIN (Employer Identification Number)**

**What:** Tax ID number for your business (like a social security number for the company)

**Needed For:**
- Opening business bank account
- Hiring employees
- Filing taxes
- Applying for business licenses

**How to Get (US):**
- Apply online at IRS.gov/EIN
- Free
- Instant (get number immediately)
- Can also apply by mail/fax (takes 2-4 weeks)

**Information Needed:**
- Legal business name
- Business address
- Type of entity
- Responsible party (name and SSN)
- Reason for applying

**Time:** 15 minutes online

**Non-US:** Check your country's tax authority (HMRC in UK, Income Tax Department in India, etc.)

---

**7. Open Business Bank Account**

**Why Separate Account is Critical:**
- Legal separation (maintain "corporate veil")
- Cleaner accounting
- Professional appearance
- Tax compliance

**What You Need:**
- EIN letter
- Articles of Incorporation/Organization
- Operating Agreement/Bylaws
- Government-issued ID
- Initial deposit ($100-$1,000 typically)

**Bank Options:**

**Traditional Banks:**
- Chase, Bank of America, Wells Fargo
- Pros: Branch access, established
- Cons: Fees ($15-$30/month), minimum balances

**Online Banks:**
- Mercury (startup-focused, no fees, no minimums)
- Novo (small business focused)
- Relay (multi-account features)
- Pros: No fees, startup-friendly
- Cons: No cash deposits, limited branch access

**Recommended:** Mercury or Relay for startups

**Time:** 1-2 hours

---

**8. Get Business Credit Card**

**Why:**
- Separate business and personal expenses
- Build business credit
- Earn rewards on business spending
- Easier expense tracking

**Startup-Friendly Options:**
- **Brex:** No personal guarantee, designed for startups with funding
- **Ramp:** Corporate card + expense management, no fees
- **American Express Business:** Requires personal guarantee, good rewards
- **Chase Ink Business:** Good for small businesses

**Time:** 30 minutes to apply

## Legal Best Practices

### 1. Work with a Startup Lawyer

**When to Hire:**
- Initial incorporation: Optional (can use Stripe Atlas/Clerky)
- Founder agreement: Highly recommended ($1,500-$3,000)
- First fundraise: Mandatory ($5,000-$15,000)
- First employee: Optional (can use templates)
- Serious legal issue: Mandatory

**How to Find:**
- Ask other founders for referrals
- Look for lawyers who specialize in startups
- Check if they offer "startup packages"
- Some work on deferred fees or small retainer

**Cost Structure:**
- **Hourly:** $300-$700/hour (typical for startup lawyers)
- **Fixed fee:** Better for defined projects (incorporation, fundraise)
- **Retainer:** Monthly fee for ongoing access

### 2. Use Standard Documents When Possible

Don't reinvent the wheel:
- YC SAFE templates
- Series Seed documents
- NVCA document templates
- Orrick startup forms library

**Why:** VCs and lawyers are familiar with standard docs = faster, cheaper closings

### 3. Actually Read Contracts

Before signing anything:
- [ ] Read it entirely
- [ ] Understand key terms
- [ ] Red-flag items (non-compete, exclusivity, auto-renewal)
- [ ] Negotiate if needed
- [ ] Get lawyer review if high-stakes

### 4. Document Everything

Use DocuSign, HelloSign, or Adobe Sign for:
- Audit trail
- Legal validity
- Organization
- Remote signing

### 5. Regular Legal Checkups

**Annually Review:**
- Corporate governance (board meetings, consents)
- Stock option grants properly documented
- IP assignments from all employees/contractors
- Customer contracts up-to-date
- Compliance with new laws (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)

## Common Legal Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå **Mistake 1: No Vesting for Founders**
- **Problem:** Co-founder leaves after 6 months with 40% equity
- **Fix:** 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff for ALL founders

‚ùå **Mistake 2: Verbal Agreements**
- **Problem:** "We agreed I'd get 30%!" (no proof)
- **Fix:** Everything in writing, signed

‚ùå **Mistake 3: Missing 83(b) Election**
- **Problem:** Huge tax bill years later when stock vests
- **Fix:** File within 30 days of stock grant, no exceptions

‚ùå **Mistake 4: No IP Assignment**
- **Problem:** Contractor claims ownership of code they wrote
- **Fix:** IP assignment agreement before work begins

‚ùå **Mistake 5: Wrong Entity Type**
- **Problem:** LLC when you need C-Corp for VC funding
- **Fix:** Research thoroughly, talk to lawyer or experienced founders

‚ùå **Mistake 6: Operating Without Business License**
- **Problem:** Fines, can't enforce contracts, looks unprofessional
- **Fix:** Research and obtain all required licenses

‚ùå **Mistake 7: Copying Terms of Service**
- **Problem:** Plagiarism, doesn't fit your business, could be illegal
- **Fix:** Use template, customize for your specific situation

‚ùå **Mistake 8: Ignoring Regulations**
- **Problem:** GDPR violation = 4% of global revenue fine
- **Fix:** Understand what applies to you, implement compliance

## Country-Specific Notes

### United States
- **Popular states:** Delaware (C-Corps), home state (LLCs)
- **Registration:** Secretary of State (each state)
- **Tax ID:** EIN from IRS
- **Startup resources:** SBA.gov, SCORE.org

### India
- **Popular entity:** Private Limited Company (Pvt Ltd)
- **Registration:** Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA)
- **Requirements:** Min 2 directors, 2 shareholders
- **Tax ID:** PAN, TAN from Income Tax Dept
- **Startup resources:** StartupIndia.gov.in

### United Kingdom
- **Popular entity:** Private Limited Company (Ltd)
- **Registration:** Companies House
- **Requirements:** Min 1 director, 1 shareholder
- **Tax ID:** UTR from HMRC
- **Startup resources:** Gov.uk/set-up-business

### Canada
- **Popular entity:** Corporation or LLC (various provinces)
- **Registration:** Federal (Corporations Canada) or Provincial
- **Tax ID:** Business Number (BN) from CRA
- **Resources:** Canada.ca/business

### Australia
- **Popular entity:** Proprietary Limited Company (Pty Ltd)
- **Registration:** ASIC (Australian Securities and Investments Commission)
- **Tax ID:** ABN, TFN from ATO
- **Resources:** Business.gov.au

### Singapore
- **Popular entity:** Private Limited Company (Pte Ltd)
- **Registration:** ACRA (Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority)
- **Requirements:** Local director required
- **Startup resources:** EnterpriseSG

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Always Consult Local Experts:** Laws vary significantly. This guide provides general information - consult licensed attorneys in your jurisdiction.
